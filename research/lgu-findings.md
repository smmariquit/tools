## 2. Per-tool findings

### A. Wages, benefits & labor (region-level: HIGH)

**Tools:** Salary Net Pay, 13th-Month, Overtime, Holiday, Separation Pay, Final/Backpay, BPO Night-Differential.

- **National baseline:** PD 442 (Labor Code) multipliers and PD 851 (13th month) are national and uniform. The variable input is the **regional daily minimum wage** set by each RTWPB under RA 6727.
- **Local deviation:** The minimum wage differs by region (and within a region by sector/area/establishment size). It drives three things in our tools:
 1. The **Minimum Wage Earner (MWE) income-tax exemption**: see below.
 2. The **floor for holiday-pay and 13th-month** computations.
 3. COLA integration where applicable.
- **MWE exemption (national rule, regional trigger):** Under **RA 9504** amending NIRC **§22(HH)** and **§24(A)(2)**, a worker paid exactly the statutory minimum wage is **exempt from income tax on the SMW plus holiday pay, overtime pay, night-shift differential, and hazard pay**. The Supreme Court (**G.R. No. 184450, 24 Jan 2017**) held that earning *other* taxable income (commissions, honoraria, side business) does **not** strip the exemption on the SMW/OT/holiday components. → A salary calc must decide "is this person an MWE?" using the *regional* wage, not a national number.
 - Source: lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2008/ra_9504_2008.html **[verified]**; G.R. 184450 lawphil.net/judjuris/juri2017/jan2017/gr_184450_2017.html **[verified]**.
- **Governing local instruments & current rates (private sector, non-agriculture top tier):**

| Region | Wage Order | Non-agri / day | Other tiers | Effective | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **NCR** | NCR-26 | **₱695** | ₱658 (agri, retail/service ≤15) | 18 Jul 2025 | nwpc.dole.gov.ph/ncr **[verified]** |
| **IV-A CALABARZON** | IVA-22 | **₱600** (top) | ₱508–₱600 range; 2-tranche | T1 05 Oct 2025, T2 01 Apr 2026 | nwpc.dole.gov.ph/region-iva **[verified]** |
| **III Central Luzon** | RBIII-26 | **₱600** (Bataan/Bulacan/N.Ecija/Pampanga/Tarlac/Zambales); Aurora ₱560 | retail/svc ₱515–₱590 | T1 30 Oct 2025, T2 16 Apr 2026 | nwpc.dole.gov.ph (RBIII-26) **[verified]** |
| **VII Central Visayas** | ROVII-26 | **₱540** Class A (Expanded Metro Cebu) / **₱500** Class B | simplified 2-tier | 04 Oct 2025 | nwpc.dole.gov.ph/region-vii **[verified]** |
| **XI Davao** | RB XI-24 | ₱515 → **₱540** (T2); agri ₱515→₱525 | 2-tranche | T1 13 Mar 2026, T2 01 Sep 2026 | nwpc.dole.gov.ph/region-xi **[verified]** |

- **Worked example (MWE flip):** A Cebu (Class B) worker at ₱500/day ≈ ₱13,042/mo is an MWE → income-tax-exempt. The identical ₱500/day in NCR is **below** the ₱695 NCR minimum, which is a labor-compliance problem, not a tax one. A flat "is ₱X exempt?" answer is wrong without the region.
- **Locally-declared holidays:** City charter days, foundation anniversaries, and fiestas declared **special non-working** by city ordinance or presidential proclamation add paid days that change holiday-pay math (special day = +30% if worked). These are per-LGU/per-year and must be cited as "check the annual Malacañang proclamation + your city ordinance," not hard-coded. **[verified: mechanism; specific dates vary yearly]**

### C. Real Property Tax / Amilyar (city-level: HIGH)

**Tool:** Amilyar / Real Property Tax Calculator.

- **National baseline (RA 7160, Book II Title II):**
 - **§233** basic RPT ceiling: **≤1% (province), ≤2% (city / MM municipality)** of assessed value. **[verified]**
 - **§218** land assessment-level *ceilings*: residential **20%**, agricultural 40%, commercial/industrial/mineral **50%**, timberland 20%, special 15%. **§219** buildings: residential **25%**, etc. LGUs may adopt **lower** levels by ordinance. **[verified]**
 - **§235** Special Education Fund (**SEF**) = additional **1%** of assessed value (mandatory add-on). **[verified]**
 - **§236** idle-land tax: optional **≤5%** of AV. **§250** quarterly installments; **§251** advance/prompt-payment discount **≤20%**. **[verified]**
 - Formula: `Assessed Value = FMV (per LGU Schedule of Market Values) × Assessment Level`; `Basic RPT = AV × rate`; total adds SEF (+ SHT/idle where applicable).
- **Local deviation: sampled cities:**

| City | Basic RPT (residential) | Basic RPT (commercial/industrial) | Notable local add-on | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Quezon City** | **1.5%** | 2% | **Socialized Housing Tax 0.5%** on assessed value of land **>₱100,000** (Ord. SP-2095, S-2011) | Inquirer / QC Revenue Code 1993 **[secondary, consistent]** |
| **Makati** | **1.5%** | 2% | +**0.25%** if residential is rented (reclassified RC); land AL only **12%** (well below 20% ceiling) | makati-eboss.ph RPT chapter PDF **[verified: official Makati eBOSS]** |
| **Manila / other MM** | up to **2%** ceiling | up to 2% | SEF 1% | RA 7160 ceiling **[verified for ceiling; per-city rate unverified]** |

- **Worked example (QC residential lot, FMV ₱1.6M):** AV = ₱1.6M × 20% = ₱320,000. At QC's **1.5%** residential rate: Basic = ₱4,800; SEF (1%) = ₱3,200 → **₱8,000/yr**. Using the generic "2% Metro Manila" assumption overstates the basic tax by ₱1,600/yr. *(Some guides cite QC at 2% under the 1993 code's commercial line; the residential 1.5% is the figure to use for homes: flag both.)*
- **SMV revisions:** Several cities have updated their Schedule of Market Values recently, which raises FMV (and thus the tax) even when the *rate* is unchanged. Cite "your city's latest SMV ordinance." **[verified: mechanism]**

### D. Zonal value / Estate / Donor's / property contracts: Local Transfer Tax (city: MEDIUM–HIGH)

**Tools:** BIR Zonal Value, Estate Tax, Donor's Tax, Legal Contract Generator (property).

- **National baseline:** CGT 6%, DST, estate tax 6%, donor's tax 6% are **national and uniform** (TRAIN). BIR zonal values are per-RDO (already inherently local in our zonal tool).
- **Local deviation: Local Transfer Tax (RA 7160 §135):** a **separate** LGU tax on transferring real-property ownership:
 - **Province: ≤0.5%** (½ of 1%); **City / MM: ≤0.75%** (¾ of 1%) of the **higher of** selling price / FMV / zonal value. **[verified]**
 - Due **within 60 days** of the deed; Register of Deeds requires proof of payment before title transfer.
- **Worked example:** ₱5.5M MM property → LTT at 0.75% = **₱41,250**; same value in a province at 0.5% = **₱27,500**. This is on top of the 6% national taxes and is frequently omitted from "total cost to transfer" estimates.
- **Estate/Donor's confirmation:** the estate/donor's *tax itself* is national 6% with no LGU variation; but settling/donating **real property** still triggers the local transfer tax above. Cross-link these tools.
 - Source: gosupra/RA 7160 §135 **[verified]**; respicio.ph transfer-tax explainer **[secondary]**.

### E. Business / professional local taxes (city: MEDIUM)

**Tools:** Freelance Tax, Freelance Rate, Influencer Rate, Food Cost, Shopee/Lazada Fee, Invoice Factoring, Architectural Fee, Customs Brokerage Fee.

- **National baseline:** income tax (TRAIN graduated / 8% option) is national.
- **Local deviations under RA 7160:**
 - **Local Business Tax (Mayor's Permit):** graduated by gross receipts, **rate set per LGU**. Ex: QC restaurants pay roughly **0.75% (¾ of 1%) on gross receipts above ₱2,000,000** plus a fixed bracket below that, *plus* Mayor's Permit, Sanitary Inspection, Garbage, and Fire (≈10% of fees) charges (QC Revenue Code). **[secondary: respicio.ph QC LBT]**
 - **Professional Tax Receipt (PTR), §139:** annual tax on professionals requiring a government exam, **capped at ≤₱300/yr**, paid to the province/city of practice, due by **31 January**; **government-employed professionals are exempt**; once paid you may practice anywhere in PH. Relevant to Architects (UAP SPP) and Customs Brokers. **[verified: gosupra/RA 7160 §139]**
 - **Barangay business tax/clearance** and amusement tax (for events/creative work) also apply per local ordinance. **[verified: mechanism]**
- **Content angle:** freelancers/creators should be told the income-tax number is only half the story: the LGU mayor's permit + (if licensed) PTR are separate annual costs that vary by city.

### F. Transport (MEDIUM)

**Tools:** LTO Registration Fee, LTO Penalty, Toll, Fuel Cost.

- **LTO MVUC & penalties are national** (RA 4136 + LTO/DOTr issuances): no LGU variation in the fee schedule itself. **[verified: mechanism]**
- **Critical E-E-A-T nuance: what users conflate with LTO:** **MMDA number coding (UVVRP)** and its fines are an **MMDA/LGU** matter, *separate* from LTO MVUC:
 - Standard NCR coding: weekdays **7:00–10:00 AM & 5:00–8:00 PM** (MMDA Resolution 22-14); window 10:01 AM–4:59 PM. Plate-last-digit days: 1/2 Mon, 3/4 Tue, 5/6 Wed, 7/8 Thu, 9/0 Fri.
 - **Makati**: continuous **7 AM–7 PM** (no window). **Baguio**: daily 5 AM–10 PM incl. weekends. **Cebu City**: suspended since 2024. **Davao City**: no coding. **Las Piñas, Parañaque, Valenzuela, Navotas**: coding suspended as of 2026.
 - Coding fine ≈ **₱300–₱500** (Single Ticketing System); unpaid blocks LTO registration renewal.
 - **NCAP (No-Contact Apprehension)** was **reinstated in early 2026** after the Supreme Court lifted the 2022 TRO (May 2025). Enforcement is CCTV/ALPR-based.
 - Sources: expressway.ph/number-coding, myltotracker.ph, philstar.com (NCAP reinstated) **[secondary, mutually consistent]**.
- **Toll: 2026 adjustments (TRB-approved):**
 - **NLEX**: final tranche of the 2023 periodic adjustment effective **20 Jan 2026** (+₱6 open system, +₱0.26/km closed; full Metro Manila↔Mabalacat +₱24 Class 1 / +₱60 C2 / +₱72 C3). **[verified: philstar/Manila Standard]**
 - **SLEX & STAR**: increase effective **1 Jan 2026** (SLEX Alabang→Calamba now ₱126 C1 / ₱253 C2 / ₱379 C3; STAR Sto.Tomas→Lipa ₱60 C1). **[verified: philstar]**
 - → Verify our `tollData.ts` matrices reflect the Jan 2026 NLEX/SLEX/STAR rates.
- **Fuel:** pump prices vary by region (freight differential: Mindanao typically higher than Metro Manila). Commercial, not statutory; note as informational. **[unverified: directional only]**

### G. Education: LGU scholarships that stack (city: MEDIUM)

**Tools:** CHED Scholarship, DOST-SEI Stipend (and GWA/Honors/QPI/PRC/CSC are institution/national: see §6).

- **National baseline:** CHED (RA 10931 free tuition in SUCs/LUCs), DOST-SEI scholarships. National and uniform.
- **Local deviation: city scholarship programs that stack on top:**

| LGU | Program | Amount | Key condition | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Quezon City** | QCSP (Ord. SP-3283, S-2024) | SHS ₱20k tuition + ₱10k stipend; **Tertiary "QC Excel" ₱110k + ₱50k**, Academic ₱80k + ₱25k; Postgrad ₱55k + ₱50k | QCitizen resident; **not receiving another LGU scholarship** | quezoncity.gov.ph QC Scholars' Guide **[verified]** |
| **Makati** | MCRSP | **₱20k–₱80k / school year** | Top 10% of SHS class; 5-yr residency; serve city 2 yrs | philscholar.com / rappler **[secondary]** |
| **Taguig** | "every SHS grad is a scholar" / LANI | **₱15k–₱50k per semester** | Taguig resident | rappler.com **[secondary]** |

- **Worked example:** A QC student on CHED free tuition can still draw the QCSP tertiary stipend; "QC Excel" alone is up to **₱160k/yr** in city aid. Students routinely miss these. (Note QC's rule barring *other LGU* scholarships: national CHED/DOST is not an LGU scholarship, so stacking with national is generally fine; confirm per cycle.)

### H. Misc

- **Shipping & Logistics:** inter-island/regional freight differentials are real (Luzon↔Visayas↔Mindanao); LGU-specific delivery constraints exist but no uniform statutory rate. **[unverified: directional]**
- **Legal Contract Generator:** property contracts trigger the §135 Local Transfer Tax + notarial + LGU clearances (see §D).
- **GCash/Maya Bill Splitter, DFA/PRC Age, ID Photo, Digital Ticket:** generic/national: no LGU angle.

## 6. Nationally uniform: NO LGU/regional variation (confirmed)

These tools have no sub-national angle; do not spend time adding LGU selectors:

- **Income Tax (TRAIN RA 10963)**: national brackets; first ₱250k exempt. *(Only the MWE flip in §A depends on region.)*
- **SSS / PhilHealth / Pag-IBIG contributions**: national schedules (RA 11199 / 11223 / 9679).
- **SSS Pension (RA 11199), GSIS Pension (RA 8291), SSS Maternity (RA 11210)**: national.
- **PDIC deposit insurance**: ₱500k/depositor/bank, national (RA 3591 as amended by RA 9576 & 11840).
- **Retail Treasury Bond yield, Digital Bank interest (20% final tax), GoTrade vs IBKR**: national/commercial.
- **Estate Tax & Donor's Tax (6%)**: national *rate*; but real-property transfer within them triggers the §135 local transfer tax (see §D).
- **De Minimis, 13th-month exemption (₱90k)**: national BIR ceilings.
- **GWA / Latin Honors / QPI-GPA / PRC Board Rating / Civil Service**: **institution-specific** (university handbooks) or national exam rules (PRC/CSC), NOT LGU law. Add per-university nuance, not per-LGU.
- **GCash/Maya Bill Splitter, DFA/PRC Age, ID Photo Maker, Digital Ticket Generator**: generic.
