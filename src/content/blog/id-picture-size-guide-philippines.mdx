Whether you're applying for a DFA Passport, a PRC License, a visa, or a standard job application, having the correct ID picture size is critical. A photo that is off by just a few millimeters will often be rejected by strict government agencies.

Instead of paying a premium at a photo studio, you can easily take and format your own ID pictures at home. Here are the exact dimensions you need.

## 1. The 1x1 ID Picture
The 1x1 is the most common requirement for school IDs, local gym memberships, and minor company records.
*   **Inches:** 1 x 1 inch
*   **Centimeters:** 2.54 x 2.54 cm
*   **Pixels (at 300 DPI):** 300 x 300 px

## 2. The 2x2 ID Picture
The 2x2 is the standard for most major government applications in the Philippines (NBI Clearance, PRC, Civil Service).
*   **Inches:** 2 x 2 inches
*   **Centimeters:** 5.08 x 5.08 cm
*   **Pixels (at 300 DPI):** 600 x 600 px

## 3. The Philippine Passport Size
This is where many people make a mistake. A Philippine Passport size photo is **not** a 2x2. It is slightly smaller and taller.
*   **Inches:** 1.37 x 1.77 inches
*   **Centimeters:** 3.5 x 4.5 cm (35mm x 45mm)
*   **Pixels (at 300 DPI):** 413 x 531 px

## General Requirements for Government IDs

To ensure your photo is accepted, adhere to these universal guidelines:
*   **Background:** Must be pure white. No shadows on the wall.
*   **Clothing:** Wear a collared shirt or decent attire. No sleeveless tops.
*   **Accessories:** Remove eyeglasses, earrings, and necklaces.
*   **Expression:** Neutral facial expression. Both ears must be visible.
