# How to Compute Your SSS Pension in the Philippines

Planning for retirement is crucial, and understanding how much you will receive from the Social Security System (SSS) is the first step. The SSS uses a specific set of formulas to determine your monthly lifetime pension. They calculate your pension using three different formulas and will automatically award you the **highest** amount among the three.

In this guide, we break down exactly how your SSS retirement pension is computed.

## The Key Factors: AMSC and CYS

To compute your pension, SSS looks at two main factors:

1. **Average Monthly Salary Credit (AMSC)**: This is the average of your last 60 months (5 years) of salary credits prior to the semester of your retirement. If your salary has been consistently maxed out (e.g., ₱30,000 to ₱35,000 depending on the current contribution table cap), your AMSC will be higher.
2. **Credited Years of Service (CYS)**: This is the total number of years you have contributed to the SSS. You need a **minimum of 10 years (120 monthly contributions)** to qualify for a regular monthly pension. If you have less than 10 years, you will only receive a lump-sum amount.

## The Three SSS Pension Formulas

The SSS computes your pension using three formulas. You don't get to choose; the system automatically grants you the highest resulting value.

### Formula 1: The Standard Formula
This formula rewards members who have contributed for many years (high CYS).
**Computation:** `₱300 + (20% of AMSC) + (2% of AMSC x [CYS - 10])`

*Example:* If your AMSC is ₱20,000 and you contributed for 25 years (CYS):
- ₱300 + (0.20 x 20,000) + (0.02 x 20,000 x 15)
- ₱300 + ₱4,000 + ₱6,000 = **₱10,300 per month**

### Formula 2: The 40% Rule
This formula is straightforward and often benefits those with a very high AMSC but fewer years of service (e.g., exactly 10 years).
**Computation:** `40% of AMSC`

*Example:* If your AMSC is ₱20,000:
- 0.40 x 20,000 = **₱8,000 per month**

### Formula 3: The Minimum Pension Rule
The SSS guarantees a minimum baseline pension depending on your years of service.
- If you have **10 to 19 CYS**, the minimum is **₱1,200**.
- If you have **20 or more CYS**, the minimum is **₱2,400**.

*(Note: There is also an additional ₱1,000 benefit typically added across the board, but the base calculation starts here).*

## How to Maximize Your SSS Pension

1. **Contribute Maximum Amounts in Your Final 5 Years**: Since the AMSC is based on the 60 months prior to retirement, maxing out your contributions during this period significantly boosts your pension.
2. **Never Stop Paying Voluntarily**: If you lose your job or become an OFW, switch your membership status to Voluntary and continue paying to increase your CYS. Every year beyond 10 years adds a 2% multiplier to your pension under Formula 1.
