During the summer months in the Philippines, electricity bills from providers like Meralco skyrocket. Many households are shocked when their bill jumps from ₱2,000 to ₱5,000 in a single month.

If you want to lower your electric bill, you need to understand exactly how much electricity each appliance consumes.

## How Electricity is Billed (The kWh)

Electricity is billed based on **Kilowatt-Hours (kWh)**. 
A Kilowatt-Hour means you used 1,000 watts of power for exactly 1 hour. 

To compute the cost of running an appliance, you need three numbers:
1.  **Wattage:** How much power the appliance uses (e.g., a non-inverter aircon uses about 1,000W).
2.  **Hours Used:** How many hours a day it runs.
3.  **Electricity Rate:** The cost per kWh charged by your provider (currently around ₱11 to ₱12 per kWh in Metro Manila).

## The Biggest Energy Hogs in Filipino Homes

1.  **Air Conditioners:** A standard 1HP non-inverter aircon running 8 hours a night can add ₱2,500 to ₱3,500 to your monthly bill alone! Switching to an inverter model can cut this cost by 40% to 60%.
2.  **Refrigerators:** Because they run 24/7, old refrigerators are massive energy drains. Always opt for high Energy Efficiency Factor (EEF) inverter models.
3.  **Electric Heaters (Shower):** Heating water takes an enormous amount of energy. A 3,500W shower heater used by a family of four can add hundreds of pesos to the bill.
4.  **Desktop Computers:** High-end gaming PCs with large power supplies can consume as much electricity as a small aircon if left running all day.

## How to Lower Your Bill

*   **Clean Your Aircon:** A dirty filter forces the compressor to work twice as hard. Clean filters every 2 weeks.
*   **Unplug "Vampire" Electronics:** TVs and chargers still consume a small amount of "standby power" even when turned off. Unplug them.
*   **Switch to LED:** LED bulbs use 80% less energy than incandescent bulbs and last significantly longer.
