# Philippine Tax Classification Guide: 8% vs Graduated Tax

Under the TRAIN Law, self-employed individuals, freelancers, and professionals in the Philippines are given a massive tax advantage: the ability to choose between the **8% Flat Income Tax Rate** and the standard **Graduated Income Tax Rate**.

Choosing the right classification can literally save you hundreds of thousands of pesos every year. Here is how they work and how to pick the right one.

## Option 1: The 8% Flat Tax Rate

The 8% flat rate is the simplest and most popular choice for freelancers and digital nomads. It replaces both your Income Tax and Percentage Tax (3%).

**The Formula:** `(Gross Sales/Receipts - ₱250,000) x 8%`

**Pros:**
- Extremely easy to compute.
- No need to keep track of receipts and business expenses.
- Replaces Percentage Tax entirely.

**Cons:**
- You **cannot** deduct business expenses. If you run a high-overhead business (like retail or manufacturing), you will be taxed on your gross revenue, which can be devastating.
- It is only available to Non-VAT taxpayers (Gross income below ₱3,000,000).

## Option 2: Graduated Tax with 40% OSD

If you choose the Graduated Income Tax, you must apply the standard BIR tax brackets (0% to 35%). However, you are allowed to deduct expenses before the tax is computed. The **Optional Standard Deduction (OSD)** allows you to automatically deduct 40% of your gross sales as "expenses" without needing to present receipts.

**The Formula:** 
1. `Taxable Income = Gross Sales - (Gross Sales x 40%)`
2. `Income Tax = Graduated Tax Table(Taxable Income)`
3. `Plus: 3% Percentage Tax on Gross Sales`

## Option 3: Graduated Tax with Itemized Deductions

If your actual business expenses exceed 40% of your gross sales, you should use Itemized Deductions. This requires you to present official receipts for rent, salaries, utilities, marketing, and supplies.

**The Formula:** 
1. `Taxable Income = Gross Sales - Actual Documented Expenses`
2. `Income Tax = Graduated Tax Table(Taxable Income)`
3. `Plus: 3% Percentage Tax on Gross Sales`

## The ₱3,000,000 VAT Threshold

It is critical to remember that the 8% Flat Rate is a privilege for small businesses. If your annual gross sales exceed **₱3,000,000**, you are mandated by law to register as a VAT Taxpayer. Once you are a VAT taxpayer, you lose the option to use the 8% flat rate and must default to the Graduated Tax system.
