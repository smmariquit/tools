# Top Digital Banks in the Philippines & Their Interest Rates

Digital banks have revolutionized saving in the Philippines. Unlike traditional banks that offer a meager 0.125% per annum, digital banks offer base interest rates ranging from **2.5% to 6.5%**, with promotional rates reaching up to **15% p.a.**

If you want to fight inflation and grow your emergency fund, parking your cash in a high-yield savings account (HYSA) is the smartest move. Here’s a guide to the top players in the market today.

- **Tonik Bank**: 4.0% p.a. (Stash) up to 6.0% (Time Deposit)

- **Komo (EastWest)**: 2.5% p.a.

## The 20% Withholding Tax

Whenever you see an advertised interest rate, remember that **it is not your exact take-home yield**. In the Philippines, the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) automatically imposes a **20% Final Withholding Tax** on interest income from bank deposits.

For example, if SeaBank advertises a **4.5% p.a.** rate:
1. Your *Gross Interest Rate* is 4.5%.
2. The BIR takes 20% of your earnings.
3. Your *Net Interest Rate* is actually **3.6% p.a.**

### Daily vs Monthly Compounding
Some banks, like SeaBank and Maya, credit your interest **every single day**. Others credit it at the end of the month. Daily compounding allows your money to grow slightly faster because the interest you earned yesterday starts earning interest today.
