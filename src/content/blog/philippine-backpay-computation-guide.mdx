# Philippine Backpay Computation Guide

When you resign or get terminated from your job in the Philippines, you are legally entitled to receive your "Final Pay," commonly referred to as **backpay**. The Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) mandates that your backpay must be released within 30 days from your date of separation.

But how exactly is your backpay computed? Here is a breakdown of the standard formula.

## What is Included in Your Backpay?

Your final pay is generally the sum of the following components, minus any outstanding liabilities:

1. **Unpaid Salary / Wages**
2. **Prorated 13th-Month Pay**
3. **Cash Conversion of Unused Leaves (VL/SL)**
4. **Tax Refunds (if applicable)**
5. **Separation Pay (if applicable)**

### 1. Unpaid Salary (Based on DOLE Divisor)
To calculate your unpaid salary for the days you worked in your final month, HR first calculates your daily rate. DOLE typically uses a divisor of **21.75 days per month** for employees working 5 days a week, or **26 days** for those working 6 days a week.

**Daily Rate** = `Monthly Salary / 21.75`
**Unpaid Salary** = `Daily Rate x Days Worked in the Final Cutoff`

### 2. Prorated 13th-Month Pay
Even if you resign in February, you are entitled to a fraction of your 13th-month pay. It is calculated proportionally based on the months you worked during the calendar year.

**Formula**: `(Total Basic Salary Earned for the Year) / 12`

### 3. Leave Conversions
Unused Service Incentive Leaves (SIL), Vacation Leaves (VL), and sometimes Sick Leaves (SL) are convertible to cash upon resignation, depending on company policy. The law mandates at least 5 days of SIL be convertible.

**Formula**: `Daily Rate x Number of Unused Convertible Leaves`

### 4. Deductions to Watch Out For
Your employer has the right to deduct liabilities from your final pay. These usually include:
- Unpaid company loans or cash advances
- SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG contributions for the final month
- Income tax due (if any)
- Penalties for AWOL or failure to complete the mandatory 30-day rendering period.

## Example Computation
Imagine you earn **₱30,000** a month. You resign on June 15 and have 5 days of unused VL. 
- **Daily Rate**: ₱30,000 / 21.75 = ₱1,379.31
- **Unpaid Salary (10 days)**: ₱13,793.10
- **Prorated 13th Month (5.5 months)**: (₱30,000 x 5.5) / 12 = ₱13,750
- **Leave Conversion (5 days)**: ₱1,379.31 x 5 = ₱6,896.55

**Gross Final Pay**: ₱34,439.65
