# Web3 Scholar-Manager Split Guide

Play-to-earn guilds depend on collaboration between **Managers** (who provide the NFTs, tokens, and capital assets) and **Scholars** (who dedicate time and effort to play and earn). To maintain trust and transparency, establishing a clear, mutually agreed-upon share split is crucial. 

Our **Web3 Scholar-Manager Split Calculator** lets both parties easily visualize and compute their respective earnings in both tokens and Philippine Pesos (PHP).

## Common Split Ratios in the Philippines

Depending on the initial capital cost of the assets and the guild's operating model, split ratios generally fall into these categories:
- **50/50 Split:** Equal division. Highly popular in games with moderate asset costs.
- **60/40 Split (Manager/Scholar):** Common when the manager provides extremely high-value NFTs or handles all gas and operational expenses.
- **30/70 Split (Manager/Scholar):** Popular in low-entry cost games or as a bonus incentive for top-tier players.

## Best Practices for Guild Managers and Scholars

1. **Clear Agreements:** Establish the exact percentage split, payment schedules (e.g., bi-weekly or monthly), and claiming methods before starting.
2. **Account for Claiming/Gas Fees:** Agree on who covers the gas fees for claiming the tokens. In most professional guilds, the manager absorbs the network fees as part of their capital contribution.
3. **Transparent Auditing:** Use our split visualizer to ensure everyone is completely aligned on the expected earnings conversion.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Who pays for the transfer gas fees?
Typically, managers handle the gas fees for claiming tokens from the game contract and dispersing them to scholars' wallets. 

### How is the token price in PHP calculated?
You can input the current coin conversion value inside our calculator. Token values can fluctuate rapidly, so it is best to calculate the split at the exact time of payout.

### Does PHTools store our payout calculations?
No. All calculations are executed locally inside your web browser. PHTools does not send any of your financial data to external servers, protecting your privacy.
