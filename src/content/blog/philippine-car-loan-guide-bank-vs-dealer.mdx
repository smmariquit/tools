When buying a brand new or second-hand car in the Philippines, one of the most critical decisions you will make isn't the color of the vehicle or the model — it's **how you will finance it**.

Unless you have ₱1.2 Million cash lying around, you will need an auto loan. And you will be faced with two main paths: **Bank Financing** or **Dealer In-House Financing**. 

Many first-time buyers fall into the dealer financing trap because it's incredibly easy, but they end up paying hundreds of thousands of pesos more. Let's break down the math.

## Bank Financing vs. In-House Financing

Here's the quick summary of the differences:

| Metric | Bank Financing | In-House (Dealer) Financing |
|---|---|---|
| **Annual Interest Rate** | **8.5% to 10.5%** | **15.0% to 22.0%** (often hidden) |
| **Down Payment Requirement** | Strict (usually 20% minimum) | Flexible (as low as 10% or "all-in" promos) |
| **Approval Speed** | 3 to 7 working days | 1 to 2 days (often instant) |
| **Requirements** | Highly document-intensive | Extremely easy (minimal vetting) |

### 🚨 The "Low Down Payment" Trap

Dealers love advertising **"All-In Low Down Payment"** promos. You might see a modern SUV advertised with a down payment of only ₱29,000. 

This sounds like an amazing deal! But here's the catch: because your down payment is tiny, the loan amount is massive. And since in-house financing interest rates are extremely high, your monthly amortization will be massive for the next 5 years. 

## The Math: A Real Comparison

Let's say you're buying a car worth **₱1,200,000** with a **20% down payment (₱240,000)**, leaving a **₱960,000 loan** for a **5-year term**:

- **Bank Financing (at 9.5% annual interest):**
  - Monthly Payment: **₱20,157 / month**
  - Total Interest: **₱249,420**
  - Total Paid: **₱1,209,420**

- **In-House Financing (at 16.5% annual interest):**
  - Monthly Payment: **₱23,598 / month**
  - Total Interest: **₱455,880**
  - Total Paid: **₱1,415,880**

### The Verdict: You save ₱206,460!

By getting pre-approved for a bank loan instead of signing up for the dealer's in-house financing, you save over **₱200,000** on interest alone. That is enough to pay for gas and maintenance for the next two years!

## How to Get the Best Deal

1. **Get Bank Pre-Approval First:** Before you even visit a dealership, apply for an auto loan at your preferred commercial bank (BPI, BDO, Security Bank, Metrobank). Banks will give you a Letter of Intent (LOI) once approved.
2. **Take the LOI to the Dealer:** Show the dealer you are paying via "Bank PO" (Purchase Order). The dealer might try to charge a "Splitting Fee" or refuse the sale (because they make massive commissions on in-house financing). Stick to your guns or go to a different dealer!
3. **Compare Chattel Mortgage & Insurance:** Banks often charge lower chattel fees and allow you to find your own comprehensive car insurance, saving you even more.
