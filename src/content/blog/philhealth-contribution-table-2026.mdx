If you've been wondering why your PhilHealth deduction feels bigger than it used to be, you're not wrong. The Universal Health Care (UHC) Act mandates a **gradual increase** in premium rates from 2019 until it reaches 5%.

As of 2026, the PhilHealth premium rate is **5.0%** of your basic monthly salary.

## How PhilHealth Premiums are Computed

The computation is refreshingly straightforward compared to SSS:

**Monthly Premium = Basic Monthly Salary × 5%**

The result is then split 50/50 between you and your employer.

### Key Numbers to Remember:
*   **Salary Floor:** ₱10,000 → Minimum monthly contribution is ₱500
*   **Salary Ceiling:** ₱100,000 → Maximum monthly contribution is ₱5,000
*   **Your Share:** Half of the total premium

If you earn ₱30,000/month, your total premium is ₱1,500, and **₱750 is deducted from your salary**.

## For Freelancers and Voluntary Members

If you are self-employed, a freelancer, or an OFW, you are classified as a **Direct Contributor** and must pay **100% of the premium yourself** (no employer to split with).

The minimum contribution for voluntary members is ₱500/month.
