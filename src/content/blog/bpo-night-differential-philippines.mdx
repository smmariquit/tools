If you work the graveyard shift at a BPO or any operation that runs through the night, the law owes you a premium called night differential, and most people underestimate it because they compute it against the wrong base. The 10 percent figure is simple. What it is 10 percent *of* is the part that quietly changes depending on whether the night happens to fall on a holiday or your rest day, and that is where the real money sits.

## What the law actually grants

Under Article 86 of the [Labor Code (PD 442)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/presdecs/pd1974/pd_442_1974.html), every hour you work between 10:00 PM and 6:00 AM earns an additional 10 percent of your regular hourly rate. It is added on top of your ordinary pay for those hours, not instead of it. If your hourly rate is ₱144.23 and you work seven hours inside the night window, the night differential is ₱144.23 × 7 × 10 percent, which is about ₱100.96, sitting on top of the ₱1,009.61 you already earned for those seven hours.

That is the ordinary-day case. The wrinkle that trips up call-center workers is that the 10 percent is calculated against the *applicable* hourly rate for that day, not always your plain base rate. Work the night shift on a regular holiday and your hourly rate for those hours is already doubled, so the night differential becomes 10 percent of the 200 percent rate. The premium scales with the day, which is exactly why a graveyard shift on Christmas is worth so much more than the headline 10 percent suggests.

<RegionalNote>
The 10 percent is national, but the hourly rate it sits on is regional. The daily minimum that anchors a minimum-wage worker's rate is set per region by the RTWPB — about **₱695/day in NCR**, **₱600 in CALABARZON and most of Central Luzon**, **₱540/₱500 in Central Visayas**, and rising to **₱540 in Davao** as of mid-2026 — so the same graveyard hour pays differently across regions. There is also a tax angle: under RA 9504 a genuine Minimum Wage Earner is income-tax-exempt on the minimum wage plus night-shift differential, holiday, and overtime pay, and "minimum wage" there means the regional figure, not a national one. Check your region's current wage order at [nwpc.dole.gov.ph](https://nwpc.dole.gov.ph/) before assuming a flat answer.
</RegionalNote>

## How it stacks with holidays and rest days

| Day Type | Base Multiplier | Night Differential base |
|---|---|---|
| Regular Day | 100% | +10% of the hourly rate |
| Rest Day | 130% | +10% of the 130% rate |
| Special Non-Working Holiday | 130% | +10% of the 130% rate |
| Special Holiday + Rest Day | 150% | +10% of the 150% rate |
| Regular Holiday | 200% | +10% of the 200% rate |
| Regular Holiday + Rest Day | 260% | +10% of the 260% rate |

Read the table as a single rule rather than six separate ones: first find the day's base multiplier, then take 10 percent of that elevated rate for the hours inside the 10PM to 6AM window. A common payroll error is computing the holiday premium correctly but pinning the night differential to the plain base rate, which shortchanges the worker on exactly the most valuable shifts of the year.

### Does night differential apply to everyone?

Night differential covers the broad class of rank-and-file employees who work the covered hours. The exceptions come from Article 82 of the Labor Code, which carves out managerial employees, field personnel whose hours cannot be determined with reasonable certainty, and domestic workers, among others. If you are a regular agent on a graveyard account, you are squarely covered, and the benefit is one you cannot validly waive. An "all-in" rate that does not break out night differential can still be questioned if the total does not actually account for it.

### Is night differential taxable?

Yes. Night differential forms part of your regular compensation, so it is subject to withholding tax and to the mandatory SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG contributions. It is not in the same protected category as the ₱90,000 worth of 13th month and other benefits, so there is no special exemption shielding it.

### What happens when I work overtime during a night shift?

The premiums stack rather than replace one another. Overtime past eight hours adds its own 25 percent premium on an ordinary day, and that compounds with the night differential for the hours that fall inside the 10PM to 6AM window. In other words you collect the overtime premium and the night differential together for those hours, which is why a long graveyard shift with overtime adds up faster than people expect.
