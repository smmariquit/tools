If you work the graveyard shift at a BPO, call center, or any company that operates at night, you are entitled to **Night Differential (ND) Pay** — an additional premium on top of your regular hourly rate.

Despite being one of the largest industries in the Philippines, many BPO workers still don't know how much extra they're actually earning for working night shifts. Let's break it all down.

## What is Night Differential?

Under **Article 86 of the Philippine Labor Code**, any employee who works between **10:00 PM and 6:00 AM** is entitled to receive an additional **10% of their regular hourly rate** for every hour of night work.

### The Formula

```
Night Differential Pay = Hourly Rate × Number of ND Hours × 10%
```

For example, if your hourly rate is ₱144.23 and you work 7 hours within the ND window (10PM-6AM):

**ND Premium = ₱144.23 × 7 × 0.10 = ₱100.96**

This ₱100.96 is **added on top** of your regular shift pay.

## How It Stacks with Holidays and Rest Days

Night Differential doesn't exist in isolation. If your night shift falls on a **holiday** or your **rest day**, the premium multipliers stack:

| Day Type | Base Multiplier | ND Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Regular Day | 100% | +10% of hourly |
| Rest Day | 130% | +10% of 130% rate |
| Special Non-Working Holiday | 130% | +10% of 130% rate |
| Special Holiday + Rest Day | 150% | +10% of 150% rate |
| Regular Holiday | 200% | +10% of 200% rate |
| Regular Holiday + Rest Day | 260% | +10% of 260% rate |

This means if you work a night shift on a **Regular Holiday**, your ND premium is based on your **doubled** rate, not your base rate!

## Common Questions

### Does ND apply to all employees?
Yes. Under DOLE rules, night differential applies to **all employees** regardless of position or pay structure, unless you fall under specific exemptions (managerial employees, field personnel, and domestic workers are exempt under Art. 82).

### Is Night Differential taxable?
Yes. ND pay is considered part of your regular compensation and is subject to **withholding tax** and mandatory contributions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG).

### What about overtime during night shift?
If you work overtime (beyond 8 hours) during a night shift, the overtime premium (25% for regular days) **stacks** with the ND premium. You get both.
