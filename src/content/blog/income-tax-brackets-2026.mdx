Every year, Filipino employees wonder: "How is my income tax computed?" The Philippine tax system uses a **graduated bracket system** under the TRAIN Law, meaning the more you earn, the higher your tax rate.

## 2026 Income Tax Brackets (Graduated Rates)

| Annual Taxable Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| ₱0 – ₱250,000 | **0% (Exempt)** |
| ₱250,001 – ₱400,000 | 15% of excess over ₱250,000 |
| ₱400,001 – ₱800,000 | ₱22,500 + 20% of excess over ₱400,000 |
| ₱800,001 – ₱2,000,000 | ₱102,500 + 25% of excess over ₱800,000 |
| ₱2,000,001 – ₱8,000,000 | ₱402,500 + 30% of excess over ₱2,000,000 |
| Over ₱8,000,000 | ₱2,202,500 + 35% of excess over ₱8,000,000 |

### The ₱250,000 Exemption

If your annual taxable income (after SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG deductions) is **₱250,000 or below** (roughly ₱20,833/month), you do NOT pay any income tax at all.

## Graduated vs. 8% Flat Rate: Which is Better?

If you are a freelancer or self-employed individual, you have the option to choose the **8% flat rate** instead of the graduated brackets.

**The 8% flat rate is better when:**
*   Your gross income is between ₱250,000 and ₱3,000,000
*   You have very few deductible business expenses

**The graduated rate is better when:**
*   You have significant business expenses that can be itemized
*   Your taxable income (after deductions) falls below the 15% bracket
