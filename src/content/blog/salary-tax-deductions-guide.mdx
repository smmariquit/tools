When you receive your first job offer in the Philippines, a ₱35,000 monthly salary sounds great. But when payday arrives, you receive much less. 

Before you get your take-home pay, both your employer and the government take a slice of the pie. These are known as **Mandatory Deductions**.

## 1. Social Security System (SSS)

Your SSS contribution is essentially a mandatory pension and insurance fund. As of 2026, the contribution rate is **15%** of your Monthly Salary Credit (MSC). 
*   **You pay:** 5%
*   **Your Employer pays:** 10%

The MSC is capped at ₱35,000. Any portion of your MSC between ₱20,000 and ₱35,000 goes into a mandatory savings program called the WISP (Workers' Investment and Savings Program), which earns tax-free dividends!

## 2. PhilHealth (Universal Health Care)

PhilHealth provides health insurance coverage for all Filipinos. The premium rate for 2026 is **5%** of your basic monthly salary, capped at a maximum salary of ₱100,000.
*   **You pay:** 2.5%
*   **Your Employer pays:** 2.5%

## 3. Pag-IBIG Fund (HDMF)

The Home Development Mutual Fund (Pag-IBIG) provides housing loans and a high-yield savings program. The maximum fund salary for computation is capped at ₱10,000.
*   **You pay:** 2% (Maximum ₱200)
*   **Your Employer pays:** 2% (Maximum ₱200)

## 4. Withholding Tax (BIR)

After SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG are deducted from your gross salary, the remaining amount is your **Taxable Income**. 

The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) applies the TRAIN Law tax brackets to this amount. If your taxable income is ₱20,833 per month or below, you are completely exempt from income tax!
