You're about to walk into a job interview and the interviewer asks: **"What's your expected salary?"**

Most Filipinos answer this with a random number, a round figure, or whatever their last salary was plus a little extra. But what if you could calculate the *exact* gross salary you need — based on your actual monthly expenses?

That's what a **Reverse Salary Calculator** does.

## The Problem with "Asking Salary"

When you negotiate a salary, you're negotiating a **gross** number. But what actually matters to you is the **net** — the money that hits your bank account after SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, and tax deductions.

Here's the gap most people miss:

| Gross Salary | Net Take-Home | Difference |
|---|---|---|
| ₱25,000 | ~₱21,500 | -₱3,500 |
| ₱35,000 | ~₱28,800 | -₱6,200 |
| ₱50,000 | ~₱39,400 | -₱10,600 |
| ₱80,000 | ~₱59,200 | -₱20,800 |

That ₱50,000 salary? After deductions, you're actually living on ₱39,400. If your rent is ₱15,000, groceries ₱8,000, and bills ₱5,000 — you barely have ₱11,000 left for everything else.

## How the Reverse Calculator Works

Instead of starting with a gross salary and seeing what's left, this tool works **backwards**:

1. **You list all your monthly expenses** (rent, food, transport, utilities, savings goals)
2. **The tool adds them up** to find your required net pay
3. **It reverse-engineers the exact gross salary** that would produce that net pay after all deductions

The math uses a binary search algorithm against the same SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, and BIR TRAIN Law tables we use in our [Salary Calculator](/salary-calculator), so the numbers are guaranteed to be consistent.

## Why This Matters for Your Career

Knowing your exact asking salary gives you a massive advantage in negotiations:

- **You're backed by math, not emotion.** When an employer pushes back on your number, you can explain exactly why you need it.
- **You won't undersell yourself.** Many Filipinos accept offers that look good on paper but can't actually cover their cost of living.
- **You can plan salary milestones.** Want to save ₱10,000/month? The tool instantly tells you what gross salary makes that possible.

## Common Expenses to Include

Don't forget these often-overlooked monthly costs:

- **Emergency Fund** (recommended: at least ₱5,000/month)
- **Health Insurance Top-Up** (HMO or private insurance)
- **Loan Payments** (SSS Salary Loan, credit card minimums)
- **Gadget/Phone Installments**
- **Subscriptions** (Netflix, Spotify, gym memberships)
- **Support for Family** (a reality for most Filipino workers)
