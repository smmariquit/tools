Whether you're resigning, being terminated, or simply curious about your December bonus, understanding how 13th Month Pay works is essential for every Filipino employee.

By law (Presidential Decree 851), **all rank-and-file employees** are entitled to a 13th Month Pay, regardless of their position, employment status, or method of computing their wages.

## The Basic Formula

**13th Month Pay = Total Basic Salary Earned During the Year ÷ 12**

Important: "Basic Salary" does NOT include overtime pay, holiday premiums, night shift differentials, or allowances. It is your raw base salary only.

### Example:
If you earned a consistent ₱25,000/month for all 12 months:
*   13th Month Pay = (₱25,000 × 12) ÷ 12 = **₱25,000**

## What if You Resign Mid-Year?

You are still entitled to a **prorated** 13th month pay. If you worked from January to August (8 months):
*   13th Month Pay = (₱25,000 × 8) ÷ 12 = **₱16,666.67**

Your employer is legally required to release this within 30 days from your last day.

## Final Pay (Back Pay) Components

When you resign or are terminated, your "Final Pay" includes more than just your last salary:

1.  **Unpaid Salary:** Pay for the days you worked in your last pay period.
2.  **Prorated 13th Month Pay:** As computed above.
3.  **Unused Vacation/Sick Leave:** If your company's policy is to convert unused leave credits to cash.
4.  **Separation Pay:** Only applicable for authorized causes of termination (e.g., retrenchment, closure). Not applicable for resignation or just cause termination.
