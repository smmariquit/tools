Every payday, a chunk of your salary goes to the Social Security System (SSS). But do you actually know how much you're paying and what you're getting in return?

Understanding your SSS contributions is critical because it determines your pension, maternity benefits, sickness benefits, and even your housing loan eligibility.

## What is the Monthly Salary Credit (MSC)?

The SSS does not use your actual salary to compute your contribution. Instead, they round your salary to the nearest **₱500** bracket. This rounded amount is called the **Monthly Salary Credit (MSC)**.

For example:
*   If you earn ₱18,300/month → your MSC is ₱18,500
*   If you earn ₱25,000/month → your MSC is ₱25,000
*   If you earn ₱100,000/month → your MSC is **still** ₱35,000 (it's capped!)

## The 2026 Contribution Rate: 15%

The total contribution is **15%** of your MSC:
*   **Employee pays:** 5%
*   **Employer pays:** 10% + EC Fee

If you are a **Voluntary or Self-Employed** member, you shoulder the full 15%.

## The WISP / MPF: Your Hidden Retirement Fund

Here's the part most Filipinos don't know about: if your MSC exceeds ₱20,000, the "extra" contribution above that threshold goes into a separate program called the **Workers' Investment and Savings Program (WISP)**, also known as the Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF).

The WISP earns **tax-free dividends** and is essentially a forced retirement savings account on top of your regular SSS pension. This is actually a very powerful benefit!
