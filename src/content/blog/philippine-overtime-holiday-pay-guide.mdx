Premium pay looks intimidating because of the multipliers, but the structure underneath is simple: figure out your hourly rate, then multiply it by whatever the day demands. The multipliers themselves are not arbitrary. They are set by the [Labor Code (PD 442)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/presdecs/pd1974/pd_442_1974.html) and consolidated in DOLE's Handbook on Workers' Statutory Monetary Benefits, which is the reference HR departments actually compute against.

## First, your hourly rate

Everything starts here, and it is the step most people skip. Your daily rate is your monthly salary divided by a working-days factor, typically 21.75 for a five-day week or 26 for a six-day week. Your hourly rate is that daily figure divided by eight. Get this wrong and every premium computed on top of it is wrong too, which is why a surprising number of payslip disputes are really just an argument about the divisor.

<RegionalNote>
The multipliers below are national, but the hourly rate they multiply is not. Your daily wage floor is set per region by the RTWPB — roughly **₱695/day in NCR**, **₱600 in CALABARZON and most of Central Luzon**, **₱540/₱500 in Central Visayas**, and rising to **₱540 in Davao** as of mid-2026 — so the same overtime hour is worth more in Metro Manila than in the provinces. There is a second regional wrinkle on the holiday side: cities declare their own special non-working days for charter days, foundation anniversaries, and fiestas, and a locally declared special day worked carries the same 30% premium as a national one. Watch both the annual Malacañang proclamation and your own city ordinance, since the local list changes every year. Confirm your region's wage order at [nwpc.dole.gov.ph](https://nwpc.dole.gov.ph/).
</RegionalNote>

## The multipliers, by kind of day

Once you know your hourly rate, the day you worked decides the multiplier applied to it.

| Type of day | First 8 hours | Overtime (beyond 8 hrs) |
|---|---|---|
| Ordinary working day | 1.00 (regular pay) | 1.25 |
| Rest day or special non-working holiday | 1.30 | 1.69 |
| Special non-working holiday falling on a rest day | 1.50 | 1.95 |
| Regular holiday | 2.00 (double pay) | 2.60 |
| Regular holiday falling on a rest day | 2.60 | 3.38 |

A few of these deserve a word. The overtime figures look oddly specific because overtime is not a flat add-on; it is 30% layered on top of the already-elevated rate for that day, which is how 1.30 becomes 1.69 rather than 1.60. The regular-holiday row also carries a rule people miss in the other direction: you are entitled to 100% of your daily wage on a regular holiday even if you do not report for work, so showing up is the difference between single pay for staying home and double pay for working. The night differential, a separate 10% premium for hours worked between 10 PM and 6 AM, stacks on top of all of this, which is why a BPO graveyard shift on a holiday produces such an eye-watering rate.

## Worked through, end to end

Say your basic hourly rate is ₱100 and you put in 10 hours on a regular holiday. The first eight hours pay at double, so 8 × ₱100 × 2.00 is ₱1,600. The two overtime hours pay at 2.60, so 2 × ₱100 × 2.60 is ₱520. The day comes to ₱2,120, against the ₱800 those same eight hours would have earned on an ordinary day.
