# Philippine Overtime & Holiday Pay Guide

Working beyond 8 hours a day, or clocking in on your supposed rest day, entitles you to premium pay under the Philippine Labor Code. The Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) mandates strict multipliers for **Overtime (OT) Pay**, **Holiday Pay**, and **Rest Day Pay**.

Here is the exact breakdown of how your premium pay is computed based on the *DOLE Handbook on Workers' Statutory Monetary Benefits*.

## Step 1: Compute Your Basic Hourly Rate

Before computing any overtime, HR first converts your monthly salary into a basic hourly rate. 

1. **Daily Rate** = `Monthly Salary / Divisor`
   *(Divisor is usually 21.75 for 5-day workweeks, or 26 for 6-day workweeks)*
2. **Hourly Rate** = `Daily Rate / 8`

## Step 2: The DOLE Premium Multipliers

Once you know your basic hourly rate, DOLE applies specific multipliers depending on *what kind of day* it is. 

### 1. Ordinary Working Day
- **First 8 Hours**: 100% (No extra pay, just your regular salary)
- **Overtime (Excess of 8 hrs)**: +25% of your hourly rate (1.25 multiplier)

### 2. Rest Day or Special Non-Working Holiday
If you work on your scheduled rest day OR a Special Non-Working Holiday (like Ninoy Aquino Day):
- **First 8 Hours**: +30% of your daily rate (1.30 multiplier)
- **Overtime**: +30% on top of the 130% rate (1.69 multiplier)

### 3. Special Non-Working Holiday AND Rest Day
If a Special Non-Working Holiday falls exactly on your scheduled rest day, and you are required to work:
- **First 8 Hours**: +50% of your daily rate (1.50 multiplier)
- **Overtime**: +30% on top of the 150% rate (1.95 multiplier)

### 4. Regular Holiday
Regular Holidays include Christmas, New Year, Labor Day, etc. If you work on these days:
- **First 8 Hours**: +100% of your daily rate (2.00 multiplier or "Double Pay")
- **Overtime**: +30% on top of the 200% rate (2.60 multiplier)
*(Note: If you DO NOT work on a Regular Holiday, you are still entitled to 100% of your daily rate).*

### 5. Regular Holiday AND Rest Day
The holy grail of premium pay. If a Regular Holiday falls on your rest day and you work:
- **First 8 Hours**: +160% of your daily rate (2.60 multiplier)
- **Overtime**: +30% on top of the 260% rate (3.38 multiplier)

## Example Computation
Let's say your Basic Hourly Rate is **₱100**. You work **10 hours** on a Regular Holiday.
- **First 8 Hours**: 8 hrs x ₱100 x 2.00 = **₱1,600**
- **Overtime (2 hrs)**: 2 hrs x ₱100 x 2.60 = **₱520**
- **Total Pay for the Day**: **₱2,120**
