Going on a road trip to Baguio, La Union, or Tagaytay? One of the biggest questions in any barkada group chat is: *"Magkano ang ambagan sa gas?"* (How much is the share for gas?)

Calculating your fuel expenses shouldn't be a guessing game. By knowing your total distance, your car's fuel efficiency, and the current gas prices, you can get a near-perfect estimate.

## The Basic Formula for Fuel Cost

To manually calculate your fuel cost, you need to follow this formula:

**1. Find the Liters Needed:**
Distance (in km) ÷ Fuel Efficiency (in km/L) = Total Liters Needed

**2. Find the Total Cost:**
Total Liters Needed × Fuel Price per Liter = Total Gas Cost

*Example:* You are driving 250km to Baguio. Your sedan gets 12 km/L, and gas is ₱65 per liter.
250 ÷ 12 = 20.83 liters.
20.83 × ₱65 = **₱1,353.95 total cost.**

## Understanding Your Vehicle's Fuel Efficiency

Different vehicles consume fuel at drastically different rates. Here is a rough guide for common vehicles in the Philippines:

*   **Motorcycles (150cc):** 25 - 40 km/L
*   **Small Hatchbacks (Wigo, Brio):** 14 - 18 km/L
*   **Compact Sedans (Vios, City):** 10 - 14 km/L
*   **Mid-size SUVs (Fortuner, Montero):** 7 - 10 km/L
*   **Large Vans (Hiace, Urvan):** 6 - 8 km/L

*Note: Heavy Metro Manila traffic can drastically lower your km/L efficiency by up to 40%. Highway driving (NLEX/SLEX) yields the best efficiency.*

## Splitting the Bill (Ambagan)

The easiest way to divide the cost is to simply divide the **Total Gas Cost** by the **Number of Passengers**. However, don't forget to factor in Toll Fees! Toll fees can sometimes cost just as much as the fuel itself.
