Selling on TikTok Shop, Shopee, or Lazada in the Philippines seems incredibly lucrative until you receive your first payout. Suddenly, your ₱1,000 item results in a net payout of only ₱750. Where did the rest of the money go?

If you are an online seller, failing to properly compute your e-commerce fees can quickly lead to bankruptcy. Let's break down the hidden costs of selling online.

## 1. The Transaction Fee (2.24%)

Both Shopee and Lazada charge a standard **Transaction Fee of 2.24%** (inclusive of VAT). This fee covers the payment gateway costs (credit cards, GCash, COD).

**The Trap:** This fee is charged on the *Total Order Amount*, which includes the Shipping Fee! If a buyer pays ₱1,000 for your item and ₱100 for shipping, the 2.24% fee is applied to the full ₱1,100, not just your ₱1,000 item.

## 2. The Commission Fee (~3% to 5%)

On top of the transaction fee, the platforms charge a Commission Fee just for letting you use their marketplace. 

*   **Shopee:** Ranges from 3% to 5% depending on your product category (e.g., Electronics might be lower, while Fashion is higher).
*   **Lazada:** Generally hovers around 3% to 5% as well.
*   **TikTok Shop:** Averages 4% to 5% for standard sellers.

*Note: Shopee Mall and LazMall sellers have completely different (and usually higher) commission structures.*

## 3. The Opt-In Program Fees (FSS & CCB)

If you've noticed the "Free Shipping" or "Cashback" badges on your competitor's products, it's because they opted into the platform's promotional programs. But these badges aren't free!

*   **Free Shipping Special (FSS):** Shopee charges an additional ~5.6% of your item price if you join this program.
*   **Coins Cashback (CCB):** Shopee charges an additional ~3.36%.
*   **TikTok Affiliate Program:** If you let creators sell for you, you will likely pay a massive 10% to 15% commission fee directly to the creator!

If you join both shipping and cashback programs, or rely heavily on TikTok Affiliates, you are giving up nearly 10% to 15% of your gross revenue on top of the base commission and transaction fees!

## The Solution: Price Accordingly

If you want to maintain a 20% profit margin, you must price your products higher to absorb these platform fees. Do not blindly copy your competitor's pricing if they are a direct manufacturer.
