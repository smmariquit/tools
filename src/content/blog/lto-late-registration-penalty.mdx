The penalty for letting your registration lapse is not a flat fine, which is the first thing people get wrong. It is a percentage of your Motor Vehicle User's Charge, so the same number of months late costs a motorcycle rider and a pickup owner very different amounts. The whole structure sits on top of the [Motor Vehicle User's Charge Law (RA 8794)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2000/ra_8794_2000.html), and the requirement to register annually in the first place comes from the [Land Transportation and Traffic Code (RA 4136)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1964/ra_4136_1964.html).

## The penalty is 50 percent of your MVUC, and it stacks

The rule is straightforward once you anchor it to the right base. A late renewal adds a surcharge of 50 percent of your annual MVUC, and that surcharge repeats for each additional year you stay lapsed. Because the MVUC itself is weight-based, your starting point depends on your vehicle class: a motorcycle sits at ₱240, a light private car up to 1,600 kilograms at ₱1,600, the 1,601 to 2,300 kilogram band at ₱3,600, and the heaviest private class at ₱8,000.

A concrete case makes the stacking obvious. Take a light sedan with a ₱1,600 MVUC that has been unregistered for two full years. You owe the registration for both years, which is ₱3,200 in MVUC, plus a penalty that has compounded to roughly the full MVUC by the second year, around ₱1,600. Add the small fixed Legal Research Fund and computer service fees and the bill lands near ₱5,000 for what would have been about ₱1,800 a year if paid on time. The penalty is not catastrophic on its own, but it is the part people never budget for.

## Vehicle weight class is the variable everyone forgets

The single most common surprise is owners assuming the penalty is the same across all vehicles. It is proportional to the MVUC, so a heavy SUV in the ₱3,600 class accrues a penalty more than twice that of a light sedan for the identical period of lateness. If you own something near a weight boundary, the gross weight printed on your OR/CR is what the surcharge is computed against, not the manufacturer's brochure figure. I have seen this catch owners who genuinely believed their "small" crossover sat in the light class when its registered weight had nudged it into the next band.

## The fine is the cheap part. Impoundment is not.

Here is the cost that does not show up in any penalty table. Driving on an expired registration is an apprehendable violation under RA 4136, which means your vehicle can be flagged at a checkpoint and impounded. Once that happens you are paying towing and daily storage fees that accumulate whether or not you are using the car, and those routinely run into the thousands and overtake the LTO penalty entirely. The rational move is to renew inside the one-month grace window the law allows before your plate-based deadline, rather than betting you will not hit a checkpoint.

<RegionalNote>
Worth separating clearly: the surcharge above is an LTO/MVUC matter, but the traffic fines people lump in with it are not. **Number coding (UVVRP)** and **NCAP** (no-contact apprehension, reinstated in early 2026) are run by the **MMDA and individual LGUs**, with coding fines of about ₱300–₱500 set per city: and their rules differ widely (Makati 7 AM–7 PM continuous; Cebu City suspended since 2024; Davao none; several NCR cities suspended as of 2026). They are not part of your registration penalty, but under the Single Ticketing System an unpaid coding or NCAP fine can still block your LTO renewal until settled. Check your own city's current coding ordinance, since these change.
</RegionalNote>

### How much is the penalty if I am only a few months late?

For a delay within the first year, the penalty is 50 percent of your MVUC, so a light car pays roughly ₱800 on top of its ₱1,600 registration. The lateness is measured against your plate-based due month, not your purchase date, which is why some people are later than they think.

### Does the penalty keep growing every year?

Yes. Past the first year the surcharge adds another 50 percent of the MVUC for each additional year lapsed, and you also owe the back registration for every year you skipped. A vehicle ignored for three or four years can owe several times its normal annual cost. The [penalty calculator](/lto-penalty-calculator) does the stacking so you walk in knowing the figure.

### Can I just keep driving and pay it all later?

You can, but you are exposed to apprehension and impoundment the entire time, and those incidental fees usually cost more than the penalty you are trying to defer. Renewing early is almost always cheaper than waiting.
