Every vehicle in the Philippines must be registered annually with the Land Transportation Office (LTO). But life gets busy, and many Filipinos end up with expired registrations.

If you're caught with an expired registration, you will face a **penalty fine** plus the hassle of not being able to legally drive your vehicle.

## How LTO Late Registration Penalties are Computed

The LTO charges a penalty of **50% of the Motor Vehicle User's Charge (MVUC)** for every year the registration is delayed.

### MVUC Rates (Annual Base Fees):
*   **Motorcycles / Tricycles:** ₱270
*   **Utility Vehicles / SUVs (up to 2,000 kg):** ₱900
*   **Cars / Sedans (up to 1,600 kg):** ₱720
*   **Trucks and Heavy Vehicles:** ₱1,500 and above

### Penalty Computation:
The penalty is 50% of the MVUC **per year** of delay.

For a motorcycle that is 2 years late:
*   MVUC: ₱270/year
*   Penalty: ₱270 × 50% × 2 years = **₱270**
*   Total to pay: 2 years MVUC (₱540) + Penalty (₱270) = **₱810**

## Tips to Avoid LTO Hassles

1.  **Set a reminder** on your phone for your registration expiry date.
2.  **Renew early.** You can renew up to 2 months before your expiration.
3.  **Check your LTO Portal.** The LTO now allows online appointment scheduling for registration renewal.
