Whether you're aiming for Latin Honors (Cum Laude, Magna Cum Laude, Summa Cum Laude), trying to maintain a scholarship, or just making sure you pass your prerequisites, knowing how to compute your **General Weighted Average (GWA)** is a vital survival skill for any college student in the Philippines.

Unlike high school grades that are simply averaged together, college grades are **weighted**. This means a 5-unit major subject affects your GWA much more than a 2-unit PE class.

## The GWA Formula Explained

To compute your GWA, you must multiply the grade of each subject by its corresponding units, add all of those products together, and then divide by the total number of units you took.

**Formula:**
`Sum of (Grade × Units) ÷ Total Units = GWA`

### Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you took three subjects this semester:
1.  **Math 101 (3 units):** Grade = 1.50
2.  **Science 101 (5 units):** Grade = 2.00
3.  **PE 1 (2 units):** Grade = 1.00

*Step 1: Multiply Grade by Units*
*   Math: 1.50 × 3 = 4.50
*   Science: 2.00 × 5 = 10.00
*   PE: 1.00 × 2 = 2.00

*Step 2: Add the Products*
4.50 + 10.00 + 2.00 = **16.50**

*Step 3: Divide by Total Units (3 + 5 + 2 = 10 units)*
16.50 ÷ 10 = **1.65 GWA**

## Different Grading Systems

It's important to note that Philippine universities use different grading scales:
*   **1.00 to 5.00 Scale (UP System, PUP):** 1.00 is excellent, 3.00 is passing, 5.00 is failing. A *lower* GWA is better.
*   **4.0 to 1.0 Scale (DLSU, UST, Ateneo):** 4.0 is excellent (A), 1.0 is passing (D). A *higher* GWA is better.
*   **Percentage Scale:** 100% is excellent, 75% is passing.
